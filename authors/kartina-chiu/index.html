Tylenol and Social Pain

 
Tough On Your Social Rejection? Can popping a pill alleviate the pain?

Whether you are injured or feel stomach pain, your doctor tells you to pop a Tylenol, and the pain will be gone within a couple of hours. The sensation of pain in your body disappears, magically.

Pain is the basic physiological response of us, humans. Over many decades, there has been a body of research on mitigating pain effects caused by external cues, such as medication, exercise and stretch, and mindfulness therapy. Amongst all, taking acetylcholine, the main component of Tylenol, is the most common method of tackling physical pain that most people have found effective given a quick onset of action time. It is particularly why acetylcholine drugs are easily accessible in most drug stores and convenience stores across the U.S. 

"I feel frasturated, painful, and sad when I am isolated, even though the introverted me inside am not good at socializing."

One of the key components of civilization is gregariousness. Ever since we are little, school education systems emphasize socializing, for example, working in groups and communicating with classmates. Social interaction seems inevitable as we are growing up, and in fact, it helps us build connections and rank the level of our social value. 

Considering social connections in school is the learning process for preparing for adulthood networking, a functional social interaction ought to be respectful, interactive, and rewarding, physically and psychologically. It is optimal that social interaction brings you many successes, but in most cases, you want to be accepted and make friends with peers. Their acceptance acts as a piece of mirror, reflecting how popular you are.
